My answer to this question: Life's short. And it is unpredictable. Death is inevitable. That's why I commit myself to make as "great" contribution as I can to the rest of the world in the very short lifetime. A classic figurative would be "I want to burn my lifetime like a candle." However, fulfilling this commitment can be no easy a job. It may end in vain, slowly deviating from its original track, being constrained by rules and self-doubt and dying from trivial matters. And a true and sacred commitment doesn't come easily. I would love to explore the fields of human cultures, religions, ethics and philosophy. And I want to share values with the world. The more elegant form of value sharing would be novel writing. To make use of my superfluous imagination I would prefer fiction composition too. I feel grateful for being alive in every single day of my life.
